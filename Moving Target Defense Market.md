**Why MTD Is Gaining Traction**

The surge in interest around moving target defense is being driven by a rising tide of sophisticated cyberattacks. Traditional static security measures are increasingly ill-equipped to handle dynamic threats, especially those that exploit predictable system configurations. MTD technologies — which continuously reconfigure system parameters or architectures — make IT environments less predictable and harder for attackers to map or compromise. In doing so, they significantly reduce the attacker’s dwell time and surface of attack. 

Government initiatives and strategic cyber-defense programs are also pushing adoption. Policymakers are recognizing the value of adaptive security, and several national-level strategies now include MTD as part of their plans to safeguard critical infrastructure. This policy support, combined with technological innovation, is giving MTD vendors a strong tailwind.

**Challenges & Risks**

Despite rapid growth potential, the report identifies some key barriers. Technical complexity is a significant inhibitor: deploying MTD often requires deep integration into existing infrastructure, careful planning, and continuous management. These demands can raise both cost and risk, especially for organizations without dedicated cybersecurity teams. 

Operational overhead can also be high. Continuously shifting system states, re-randomizing network parameters, or reconfiguring endpoints needs reliable automation and orchestration — without disrupting legitimate business processes. 

Regulatory and governance complexity presents another challenge. While standards are emerging — such as the adoption of adaptive controls in NIST CSF — companies must carefully design MTD deployments so that they meet compliance and audit requirements. 

Finally, not every organization is ready to absorb the upfront investment in MTD. For smaller companies or SMEs, the cost-to-benefit ratio may appear unfavorable without clear risk quantification or pilot deployments.
